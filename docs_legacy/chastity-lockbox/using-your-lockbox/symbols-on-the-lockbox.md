# Symbols on the Lockbox

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/header.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The header bar of the Lockbox shows several symbols which indicate the state of your lockbox. Here's exactly what each of those symbols means.

From left to right the icons are:

{% stepper %}
{% step %}

#### The Lock Icon

This indicates if your device is currently locked, and the status indicator tells you if the backplate is on. For more details see here [#lock-icon](symbols-on-the-lockbox.md#lock-icon "mention")
{% endstep %}

{% step %}

#### The Wi-Fi Icon

This indicates if you're device is currently connected to a Wi-Fi network. The status idicator tells you if you're also connected to our dashboard and ready to play. More details can be found here [#wi-fi-icon](symbols-on-the-lockbox.md#wi-fi-icon "mention")
{% endstep %}

{% step %}

#### The Battery Icon

This icon will show you the current battery level of your device. It will also show you if you're currently charging your device. There is no status indicator on this icon. More details can be found here [#battery-icon](symbols-on-the-lockbox.md#battery-icon "mention")
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## Lock Icon

The lock icon shows you the current status of the sensors on your device. This icon does not indicate anything about your active play session.

### Locked

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/locked.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

When you see this symbol it means that the locking mechanism is in the "locked state". This means that your device's sensors has detected that it is locked and cannot be opened.

### Unlocked

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/unlocked.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Likewise, the unlocked symbol means that the sensors on the device have detected the lock is open and you can access the contents of the box.

### Backplate On

<div><figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/locked backplate on.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/unlocked backplate on.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

In the bottom left of your Lock Icon you can see a small status indicator. When this status indicator is green this means that the sensors on your device have detected the backplate is in position, and that we're ready to lock. If can be shown for Locked or Unlocked states.

### Backplate Off

<div><figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/locked backplate off (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/unlocked backplate off.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

If the status indicator for the Lock Icon is solid yellow, then this means that the sensors of the device have determined that the backplate is currently off. Attempting to lock in this state will fail.

## Wi-Fi Icon

### Wi-Fi Not Connected

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/wifi-off (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This symbol indicates that your device is not connected to Wi-Fi at all. This might be the case if:

- Your lockbox is brand new and you have not connected your Lockbox to Wi-Fi ever
- You have connected your lockbox to Wi-Fi but you're now out of range of that network

In both of these cases you'll need to configure Wi-Fi as described in this guide [step-1-connect-your-lockbox-to-wi-fi.md](../quick-start/step-1-connect-your-lockbox-to-wi-fi.md "mention")

### Wi-Fi Connected

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/wifi (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you see this symbol, then great news! You are connected to Wi-Fi correctly! This means that your device can make requests to the internet.

But to receive commands you'll need to pair your device and connect it to the dashboard. For help with that, please follow this guide [pairing-your-chastity-lockbox.md](../quick-start/pairing-your-chastity-lockbox.md "mention")

You can always tell if your device is ready to receive commands by looking at the status indicator to the bottom left of the Wi-Fi icon.

#### Status Indicator: Flashing Yellow

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/wifi-flashing.webp" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you are connected to Wi-Fi and your status indicator is flashing yellow, then your device is connected to the internet but it is not connected to the dashboard. This means that your device is not ready to receive commands from the dashboard.

This can occur if your device has not been paired to the dashboard, if that's the case, then please follow this guide: [pairing-your-chastity-lockbox.md](../quick-start/pairing-your-chastity-lockbox.md "mention").

This can also occur if your device has recently been powered on, and it attempting to access our network. Typically this takes 15-30 seconds, however it can take up to 2 minutes to establish a stable connection (depending on where you are in the world. Sorry Antarctica ❄️)

If you have waited two minutes and you're still seeing the yellow flashing dot, then please contact support, as this might indicate a problem with our server.

{% include "../../.gitbook/includes/contact-us.md" %}

#### Status Indicator: Solid Green

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/wifi-solid-green.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you are connected to Wi-Fi and your status indicator is solid and green, then this indicates that you're online, and connected to the dashboard. Your device is ready to receive commands instantly! Have fun!

## Battery Icon

<div><figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/battery charging.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/battery full.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/battery mid.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/battery low.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/battery none.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

These icons show you the relative battery state of your device. If it's red, please charge it right away! You'll know your device is charging when you see the lightning bolt through the battery icon.
