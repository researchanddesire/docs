## Create a share request

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to Partner settings">
  Go to your **Settings** page and find the **Partners** section.
</Step>

<Step title="Create an invitation">
  Click the **Create Invitation** button in the top right corner.
</Step>

<Step title="Enter partner details">
  Enter your partner's email address or username. Choose whether you want to:
  - **Give them access** to your devices
  - **Request access** to their devices
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
For privacy and security, we won't confirm whether a user exists in our system. Double-check the spelling of their email or username to ensure your invitation reaches them.
</Note>

## Manage your connections

Once you've established connections, you'll see your partners listed in the **Partners** section. Each connection displays an indicator showing the relationship type:

| Icon | Meaning | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| ↗️ | You're sharing | You've given them control of your devices |
| ↙️ | They're sharing | They've given you access to their devices |
| ↔️ | Mutual sharing | Both partners can control each other's devices |

<Tip>
If you and your partner want to switch roles, you can set up mutual sharing. Learn more about this configuration in our guide.
</Tip>

<Card title="Set up a switch account" icon="arrows-rotate" href="how-to-set-up-a-switch-acount">
Configure mutual device sharing for partners who switch roles.
</Card>

To manage a connection, click the menu next to a partner's name. Select **Remove Partner** to end the sharing arrangement.

## Respond to invitations

When someone invites you to share device access, you'll receive:

1. An email notification
2. A pending invitation in your **Settings** page under **Pending Invitations**

Review each request carefully. You can **accept** to establish the connection or **decline** if you're not comfortable with the arrangement.

## Best practices

<Warning>
Only share device access with people you know and trust. You maintain full control of your sharing preferences and can remove access at any time.
</Warning>

- Communicate clearly with your partner about expectations before sharing
- Review your connected partners periodically
- Remove connections you no longer need
