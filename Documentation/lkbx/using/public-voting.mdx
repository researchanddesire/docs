title: "Public Voting"
description: "Let others vote to increase or decrease your lock time for added excitement and community engagement"
---

Public voting lets you share an active lock session so others can vote to increase or decrease your remaining lock time. This introduces friendly unpredictability and community interaction while you stay in control of the rules.

<Info>
  Public voting only applies to active lock sessions. If your session ends or is abandoned, the public voting link becomes inactive.
</Info>

## How it works

- You enable public voting for a specific active lock session.
- Voters choose to increase or decrease your remaining time.
- Each vote adjusts the timer by fixed amounts you configure.
- You can disable public voting at any time.

<Tip>
  Start with small, balanced increments (for example, +60 seconds and −60 seconds) to keep sessions engaging without extreme swings.
</Tip>

## Enable public voting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your session or template settings">
    On the "Templates" part of the app, click "Edit" on an existing lockbox template, or click "New lockbox template". For lockbox sessions that are already active, if you are the keyholder or if it is a test session, you can click "Modify settings" on that session in the "Home" page of the Chastity Lockbox part of the dashboard.

    <Check>
      You should see a setting labeled "Public Voting" within the session or lockbox settings.
    </Check>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the Public Voting option">
    Toggle on <strong>Public Voting</strong>
     for the active lock session.

    <Warning>
      Public voting affects your timer in real time. Make sure you are comfortable with the configured increments before enabling it.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure time values">
    Click on the cog icon next to the public voting toggle, and define how each vote changes your time:

    - <strong>Time Plus</strong>
      : Seconds added for an increase vote (👍)
    - <strong>Time Minus</strong>
      : Seconds removed for a decrease vote (👎)
    - By default, voters can reduce or increase your lock session's duration without limits. If you would like public voting to only be permitted to increase or decrease the lock duration up until certain upper (max) and lower (min) limits, you can specify that in the "Maximum Lock Duration" and/or "Minimum Lock Duration" sections on this screen.

    <Note>
      Time values are in seconds. Use whole numbers only.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Maximum and minimum lock duration (voting limits)

**Maximum Lock Duration** and **Minimum Lock Duration** are **bounds on how far public voting can move your session**—they are not an “extra days on top of your base lock” allowance in isolation. If you set a maximum, voters cannot push your session time **above** that ceiling—so if your session is already at that limit, **increase** or **extend** options on the public voting page can be **disabled or greyed out**.

<Warning>
  **Self-locks** cannot edit active session settings in the dashboard. Plan your public voting limits **before** you start the lock. If you misconfigured a setting and need it changed after the session began, contact [support@researchanddesire.com](mailto:support@researchanddesire.com)—whether that can be adjusted depends on your session type and policy.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  If you want a long window for voters to add time, set a **maximum** high enough that your session still has “room” to grow under public voting. If the maximum is too low relative to your current remaining time, increase votes may never apply.
</Tip>

## Share your voting link

Once public voting is enabled on an active session, a unique share link becomes available.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your lockbox page">
    Navigate to the lockbox page in your dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Public Voting badge">
    Look for the <strong>Public Voting</strong>
     badge with a share icon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy and share your link">
    Select the badge to copy your public voting link, then share it with friends, partners, or your community.

    <Check>
      Open the link in a private/incognito window to confirm it loads the public voting view without exposing your account details.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## View voting activity

You can track votes in two places.

### Vote counter badges

On the lockbox page, counters appear next to session details when public voting is active or after receiving the first vote:

- 👍 Thumbs Up: Total votes to increase time
- 👎 Thumbs Down: Total votes to decrease time

<Note>
  Counters only display when public voting is enabled or at least one vote has been recorded for the session.
</Note>

### Activity history

For a detailed view of votes and other events:

1. Open your lockbox page.
2. Find the <strong>Session activity</strong>
    section.
3. Select <strong>View more</strong>
    to open the full activity log.

Each vote event shows:

- <strong>Icon</strong>
  : 👍 for increase, 👎 for decrease
- <strong>Action</strong>
  : "Voted to increase time" or "Voted to decrease time"
- <strong>Voter</strong>
  : Username (or "Anonymous" if the voter was not logged in)
- <strong>Timestamp</strong>
  : When the vote was cast

Vote events appear alongside other session activities (locks, unlocks, breaks) in chronological order.

## Voting restrictions

To reduce spam and abuse, the following limits apply:

- Each person can vote once every 24 hours on a given session.
- Votes are tracked by user account and by IP address.

<Info>
  If both an account and an IP are detected for the same person, the stricter limit is applied.
</Info>

## Privacy and anonymity

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Logged‑in voters">
    When someone votes while logged in, their username appears in your activity history.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Anonymous voters">
    When someone votes without logging in, they appear as "Anonymous" in your activity history.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Your privacy">
    Your public voting link shows session details necessary for voting but does not expose your account credentials or private profile information.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Both you and voters earn points for participating in public voting.

<CardGroup cols={2}>

  </Card>
  <Card icon="star" title="Voters">
    Earn points when they vote on sessions.

<Info>
  Point values decrease throughout the day as more votes are cast, which encourages early participation.

## Tips for engagement

- Share your link with communities that match your interests and boundaries.
- Use balanced increments to keep the session fun and sustainable.
- Check your activity history regularly to see who is supporting (or challenging) you.
- Remember: votes apply immediately. Share responsibly.

## Disable public voting

You can turn public voting off at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open session settings">
    Go to your active lock session settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn off Public Voting">
    Toggle off the <strong>Public Voting</strong>
     option.

    <Check>
      The Public Voting badge and share link should no longer appear on the lockbox page.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Disabling public voting stops new votes from being accepted. Past vote events remain in your activity history.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I don’t see the Public Voting toggle">
    Ensure you’re viewing an active lock session. The toggle only appears for active sessions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The link shows as inactive">
    The session may have ended or been abandoned. Public voting links only work for active sessions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="People say they can’t vote again">
    Voters are limited to one vote per session every 24 hours. Ask them to try again after the cooldown period.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Votes seem too extreme">
    Lower the Time Plus/Time Minus values to more moderate increments.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Increase time or extend is greyed out on the public voting page">
    Your session is likely already at the **Maximum Lock Duration** you configured for public voting (voters cannot add time beyond that ceiling), or another rule prevents further increases. Review the [Maximum and minimum lock duration](#maximum-and-minimum-lock-duration-voting-limits) section above. For **self-locks**, you cannot raise that ceiling yourself after the session starts—see [What is a self-lock?](/lkbx/faqs#what-is-a-self-lock) and contact support if you need help.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
