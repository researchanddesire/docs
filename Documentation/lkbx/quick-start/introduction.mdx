Welcome to Chastity Lockbox! The Lockbox is a smart key‑holding device designed for self‑keyholding and remote play with a partner—from anywhere in the world.

<Info>
  For the best experience, use the online dashboard to configure locks, invite keyholders, and manage sessions. You can still lock and track time offline directly on the device.
</Info>

## Prerequisites :)

- A USB‑C cable that supports data transfer (not just charging)
- Have access to a 2.4 GHz Wi‑Fi network (5 GHz is not supported)
- Optional: Create your dashboard account at [dashboard.researchanddesire.com](https://dashboard.researchanddesire.com)

## Turning on your Lockbox

<Steps>
  <Step title="Charge your Lockbox">
    Connect your Lockbox to a power source using the included USB‑C cable. A lightning bolt appears on the battery icon when charging.

    <Tip>
      For best results, use a USB port on your computer or a standard phone charger (5V). Leave the device charging for at least 30 minutes before first use.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wake the device">
    Press the **Enter/Lock** button (right side) to wake the device. The screen should illuminate and display the main menu or startup screen.

    <Info>
      New devices may ship in deep sleep mode. If the screen doesn't turn on immediately, keep the device plugged in and try pressing the Enter button again after a few minutes of charging.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the device is working">
    You should see the Lockbox startup screen showing the firmware version, followed by the main menu with options like Lock, Lock Forever, Settings, and Sleep.

    <Check>
      If you see the main menu, your Lockbox is ready for Wi‑Fi setup.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If your device doesn't turn on after charging for 30 minutes, see the <a href="/lkbx/faqs#my-lockbox-wont-turn-on">troubleshooting guide</a>

  for next steps.
</Warning>

## Set up Wi‑Fi

<Info>
  If you’re using **automatic Bluetooth pairing** (Chrome or Edge), you can skip this step — WiFi is configured for you during pairing.
</Info>

For manual pairing, connect your Lockbox to WiFi first:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Wi‑Fi setup on the device">
    Use the device menu to navigate to Settings → Wi‑Fi Settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code with your phone">
    When the QR code appears on the Lockbox screen, scan it to open the setup page on your phone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your network and enter the password">
    Select your 2.4 GHz Wi‑Fi network and enter the password to connect.

    <Warning>
      The Lockbox requires a 2.4 GHz connection. 5 GHz networks aren’t supported.
    </Warning>

    <Check>
      Look for a green dot next to the Wi‑Fi icon on the Lockbox display to confirm you’re online.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you prefer a deeper walkthrough, see <a href="/lkbx/quick-start/wifi-setup">Step 1: Connect to Wi‑Fi</a>

  .
</Tip>

## Pair with your dashboard account

Pairing lets you control locks remotely, assign a keyholder, and use advanced features.

**The fastest way to pair is via Bluetooth** (Chrome or Edge only). It links your account and configures WiFi in one step.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in or register">
    Open the dashboard and sign in. If you don’t have an account yet, create one at [dashboard.researchanddesire.com](https://dashboard.researchanddesire.com).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the pairing flow">
    Go to **Settings → Devices** and click **Pair Lockbox**.

    - **Automatic tab** (recommended): Click **Scan for Lockbox**, select your device from the browser picker, and follow the prompts. Works in Chrome and Edge.
    - **Enter Code tab**: Enter the six-digit code shown on your Lockbox screen under **Settings → Pairing**. Works in any browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the device appears in your dashboard">
    You should see your Lockbox listed and ready to control.

    <Check>
      From the Chastity Lockbox section in the dashboard, confirm the device shows as Online.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Need more guidance? See <a href="/lkbx/quick-start/pairing">Step 2: Pair your Lockbox</a>

  .
</Tip>

## Start your first lock

You can start a lock on‑device or from the dashboard.

<Steps>
  <Step title="From the device (works offline)">
    Choose Lock on the device, set the duration, and confirm. The timer starts immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="From the dashboard (recommended)">
    Use the Create Lock flow in the Chastity Lockbox section to set duration, optional breaks, and keyholder rules. See <a href="/lkbx/quick-start/start-a-lock">How to start a lock from the dashboard</a>

    .
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally hide the remaining time">
    For extra challenge, you can hide remaining time across the device and dashboard. Learn how in <a href="/lkbx/using/hiding-time">Hiding the remaining time</a>

    .
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Lock modes

### Lock (offline capable)

Lock immediately for a fixed duration—even without Wi‑Fi.

### Lock Forever (online only)

Defers all unlock decisions to your dashboard rules and keyholder. Only your online settings or your keyholder can release the lock.

<Warning>
  Be careful—if you enable Lock Forever without a plan, you may be unable to unlock without your keyholder.
</Warning>

### Sleep mode

Use Sleep to conserve power when running on battery. The timer continues while offline. For details on automatic vs. deep sleep, see <a href="/lkbx/device-states/sleep-mode">Sleep mode</a>

.

## Unlocking

The device unlocks only when one of the following is true:

- The timer expires
- Your keyholder unlocks it from the dashboard

<Tip>
  Keyholders can unlock at any time regardless of the remaining timer.
</Tip>

## Emergency unlocking

### Software Emergency Unlock

If you need urgent access, press and hold the enter/lock button to open the menu, then select Emergency Unlock and confirm.

<Info>
  Using Emergency Unlock ends the current session immediately. Additional consequences and dashboard actions may apply; see the latest guidance on <a href="/lkbx/device-states/emergency-unlock">Emergency Unlock</a>

  .
</Info>

### Hardware Emergency Unlock (last resort)

If the device fails or has no power, you can mechanically open it via the backplate access port.

<Warning>
  This method is destructive and requires replacing the back cover. Only use it if software emergency unlock isn’t available.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

### Wi‑Fi connection

- Move closer to your router during setup
- Confirm your network is 2.4 GHz
- Test your password on another device

### Restarting the device

- Use the menu: Settings → Restart Device
- Hard reset (for unresponsive devices): Press and hold the Enter/Lock button for 10 seconds

## Safety reminders

<Note>
  - Practice safe chastity and good hygiene so you can stay locked longer, safely
  - Always maintain a backup release method
  - Keep your Lockbox charged when possible for the most reliable experience
</Note>
