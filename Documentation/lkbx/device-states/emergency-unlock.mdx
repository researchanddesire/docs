---
title: "Emergency Unlock"
description: "Use the Emergency Unlock safety feature and understand what happens when you do"
---

Emergency Unlock is a safety feature that lets you open your Lockbox locally when you need immediate access and your Keyholder isn’t available. Use it only in genuine emergencies.

<Warning>
Emergency Unlock is for real emergencies. Using it without a valid reason may damage trust with your Keyholder and affect your experience.
</Warning>

## Use Emergency Unlock

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the device menu">
    While the Lockbox is locked or a lock timer is running, press and hold the Lock/Enter button for about 2 seconds until the menu appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Emergency Unlock">
    Navigate to <b>Emergency Unlock</b> and select it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Confirm your decision. A QR code linking back to this guide appears on the screen.

    <Check>
      Verify success:
      - The device displays that it is unlocked and the lid can be opened.
      - In your dashboard, the active lock session shows as ended.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens when you use it

Using Emergency Unlock triggers important consequences:

1. **Immediate session end** — Your current lock session ends right away.
2. **Partner notification** — If you have a Keyholder or partners linked to your account, they receive an alert via email and the dashboard.
3. **24‑hour cooldown** — After an emergency unlock, the Lockbox cannot start a new lock for 24 hours. You may see “Emergency Mode” during this period. After the cooldown ends, locking becomes available again.

<Info>
Activity related to Emergency Unlock appears in your device’s activity history in the dashboard.
</Info>

## Before you use Emergency Unlock

<Tip>
Try messaging your Keyholder to request a break first. Open communication is usually the best path.
</Tip>

Consider these checks before proceeding:

- Confirm the situation is a genuine emergency requiring immediate access
- Try contacting your Keyholder directly for a temporary break
- Remember you may be asked to explain your reasons afterward

## Disable Emergency Unlock (optional)

If you find Emergency Unlock too tempting, you can disable it from your dashboard so it cannot be used under any circumstances.

<Warning>
When Emergency Unlock is disabled, the only way to access the device without your Keyholder is to physically break the backplate. Consider this carefully.
</Warning>

### What to know

- You can change this setting only while the device is unlocked
- Once disabled, Emergency Unlock cannot be used
- The only way to unlock will be through your Keyholder

### How to disable it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your dashboard">
    Sign in and go to your Lockbox dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the session settings">
    Navigate to your current Lock session settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate Emergency Unlock">
    Find the <b>Emergency Unlock</b> section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle to disable">
    Toggle <b>Disable Emergency Unlock</b> and confirm.

    <Check>
      Verify the toggle remains enabled after refresh.
    </Check>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Disabling adds commitment and security, but it also means you cannot access the device in a real emergency without your Keyholder’s help.
</Note>

## If the software menu isn’t available

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Emergency Unlock is disabled or blocked">
    If your Keyholder or lock template disabled Emergency Unlock, the software option won’t appear. As a last resort, you can perform a hardware emergency unlock by breaking the backplate. See: <a href="/chastity-lockbox/support/how-to-do-an-emergency-unlock-by-breaking-the-backplate">How to do an Emergency Unlock by breaking the Backplate</a>.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Device is unresponsive">
    Restart the device, then try again: <a href="/chastity-lockbox/technical-documentation/device-states/restarting-device">Restarting Device</a>.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No internet connection">
    Emergency Unlock works locally on the device and does not require an internet connection. If Wi‑Fi is down, you can still use the device menu.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
