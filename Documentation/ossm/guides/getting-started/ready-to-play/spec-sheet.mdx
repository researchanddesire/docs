## Overview

These specifications help you plan power, performance, and mechanical modifications for OSSM Ready to Play. Values represent typical performance of a stock unit under normal operating conditions.

<Info>
  Unless otherwise noted, measurements reflect operation with a dedicated 24 V DC, 4 A power supply. Actual performance can vary with payload, friction, alignment, and firmware settings.
</Info>

## Electrical

| Specification     | Value   |
| ----------------- | ------- |
| Operating voltage | 24 V DC |
| Current draw      | 4 A max |
| Idle power        | 1–5 W   |
| Average power     | 30 W    |
| Peak power        | 95 W    |

<Warning>
  Use a quality 24 V DC power supply rated for at least 4 A to achieve peak performance. Undersized or unstable supplies can cause stalls, reduced force, or controller resets.
</Warning>

## Performance

| Specification                         | Value                       |
| ------------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Maximum linear speed                  | 1 m/s (3.3 ft/s)           |
| Maximum strokes per minute            | ~165 SPM (at full stroke)   |
| Maximum pushing force (24 V supply)   | 45 lbs (20 kg)              |
| Maximum pushing force (USB‑C adapter) | 35 lbs (16 kg)              |
| Maximum toy weight                    | 2.5 lbs (1.1 kg)           |

<Note>
  **Toy mass vs motor torque:** Maximum toy **weight** is a mechanical limit for the mounted assembly (including static load on the actuator). It is separate from **pushing force** and optional **motor upgrades**, which address torque during motion (resistance, demanding patterns). A motor upgrade does **not** increase the maximum rated toy weight.
</Note>

<Note>
  Strokes per minute varies with stroke length. The maximum SPM assumes a full 7" stroke at maximum speed. Shorter strokes allow higher SPM; longer strokes (with an extended rail) reduce maximum SPM proportionally.
</Note>

<Note>
  A USB‑C power adapter delivers reduced pushing force compared to a dedicated 24 V supply. For applications requiring maximum force, use the full 24 V @ 4 A power supply.
</Note>

## Storage and Portability

The Ready-to-Play stand folds flat for compact storage—slide it under a bed or into a closet. The unit does not disassemble, but the collapsible design makes it easy to store when not in use.

## Noise

OSSM uses a nearly silent belt drive system. At low to moderate speeds, the machine operates very quietly. Noise increases at higher speeds but remains reasonable for most environments.

## Modifications

You can extend the rail length beyond the default configuration to increase stroke length.

| Modification                    | Limit                       |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Maximum recommended rail length | 18 in (450 mm)              |
| Maximum stroke length           | Rail length − 7 in (180 mm) |

<Tip>
  Calculate your maximum stroke by subtracting 7 in (180 mm) from your total rail length. For example, an 18 in rail supports a maximum stroke of 11 in.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Extending rail length increases the potential stroke but may also increase bending and friction if not properly supported. Ensure rails are aligned and supported to maintain smooth motion and reduce wear.
</Note>

### Upgrading your rail later

If you purchase a Ready to Play with the standard 350mm rail and decide you want more stroke length later, you can upgrade. The rail is designed to be user-replaceable—your R2P includes spare fasteners and Allen keys to support modifications.

To order a longer rail:

1. Contact [support@researchanddesire.com](mailto:support@researchanddesire.com) with your upgrade request
2. Specify your preferred rail length (400mm or 500mm are standard stock sizes; other lengths may require additional lead time)
3. Support will provide pricing and shipping details

<Tip>
  Rail replacement is straightforward and doesn't require specialized tools beyond the Allen keys included with your kit. If you're unsure about the process, reach out to support or ask in the Kinky Makers Discord.
</Tip>

## Custom Order Options

When ordering your Ready to Play unit, you can request the following customizations:

| Upgrade                       | Description                                                                   |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Extended rail                 | Longer rail for increased stroke length (up to 500mm)                         |
| Wider belt                    | 12mm belt for increased durability and power transfer                         |
| Motor upgrade                 | Higher-torque motor for demanding motion (resistance, high load)—does **not** raise the maximum rated toy **weight** above 1.1 kg |
| Motor color                   | Black motor instead of the standard gold finish                               |
| Custom 3D printed part colors | Choose a custom finish for all 3D printed components—**single color** (e.g. all black), multi-color, or other combinations. Say whether you want **no contrasting accent** pieces if that matters to you |
| Bottom-mounted actuator       | Actuator assembled on the **downward-facing** side of the extrusion arm (instead of the upward-facing side) so the thrust rail sits lower—useful for low-clearance setups such as lying on a bed. An additional fee applies. **Storage:** the stand may **not** fold to the usual fully collapsed size; footprint when folded is typically between standard collapsed and upright dimensions—ask support for an estimate when you order. |

<Tip>
  For **large** toys in terms of girth or length, or for **high-resistance** play where the machine works hard against friction or muscle pressure, we recommend requesting the wider belt and, when appropriate, a motor upgrade. Those options improve motion under load; they do **not** increase the maximum rated **toy mass** in the performance table.
</Tip>
