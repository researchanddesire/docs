If your Chastity Lockbox or Deepthroat Trainer isn't connecting to Wi‑Fi, use this guide to diagnose and fix the problem quickly.

<Warning>
R+D devices require a 2.4 GHz Wi‑Fi network. They are not compatible with 5 GHz–only networks.
</Warning>

<Tip>
Place the device next to your router while troubleshooting. This rules out weak signal strength and interference.
</Tip>

<Note>
- Make sure your router provides internet access (not just local LAN access).
- Guest or captive‑portal networks (those that open a web page to sign in) are not supported for device connectivity.
</Note>

## How the Lockbox shows Wi‑Fi status

The Chastity Lockbox uses a Wi‑Fi icon and a colored status circle on the display.

### Wi‑Fi icon

- Wi‑Fi icon off: The device is not connected to Wi‑Fi.
- Wi‑Fi icon on (no slash): The device may be connected — check the colored circle for details.

## Troubleshooting steps

Work through these steps in order. After each step, check your device's status circle.

<Steps>
<Step title="Confirm network requirements">
- Ensure you have a 2.4 GHz Wi‑Fi network available (many routers broadcast both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz; connect to the 2.4 GHz SSID).
- If your router combines 2.4/5 GHz under one name (band steering), temporarily split them into separate SSIDs and connect to the 2.4 GHz SSID.
- Use WPA2‑PSK (AES) security or WPA2/WPA3 mixed mode. Avoid WPA3‑only, WEP, or WPA‑TKIP.
- Disable guest/captive‑portal sign‑in for the device's network.

<Check>
You should see the Wi‑Fi icon turn on. If a green circle does not appear yet, continue to the next step.
</Check>
</Step>

<Step title="Connect your device to Wi‑Fi">
<Tabs>
<Tab title="Chastity Lockbox">
1. On the Lockbox, go to Settings → Wi‑Fi Settings.
2. Scan the QR code shown on the Lockbox display with your phone.
3. On your phone, select your 2.4 GHz network and enter the Wi‑Fi password.

<Check>
A green dot appears to the left of the Lockbox Wi‑Fi symbol when connected. If you don't see the green dot, double‑check the password and try again.
</Check>

<Info>
See Quick Start for setup basics: <a href="/chastity-lockbox/quick-start">Lockbox Quick Start</a>. FAQs also cover Wi‑Fi behavior and indicators: <a href="/chastity-lockbox/faqs">Lockbox FAQs</a>.
</Info>
</Tab>

<Tab title="Deepthroat Trainer">
1. Power on the Trainer. When prompted, hold the button to open the Settings page.
2. The Trainer starts a temporary Wi‑Fi network named "Trainer Setup."
3. On your phone, join the "Trainer Setup" network. A setup page opens.
4. Tap Configure Wi‑Fi, choose your 2.4 GHz network, and enter the password, then Save.
5. The Trainer will restart and connect.

<Check>
After restart, the Trainer connects and downloads settings automatically. If it doesn't connect, repeat the steps and confirm you selected a 2.4 GHz SSID.
</Check>

<Info>
Detailed walkthrough: <a href="/deepthroat-trainer/quick-start/pairing-your-trainer/step-by-step-guide-for-connecting-trainer-to-wi-fi">Connect Trainer to Wi‑Fi</a>
</Info>
</Tab>
</Tabs>
</Step>

<Step title="Restart your network equipment">
Unplug your modem and router for 30 seconds, then plug them back in. Wait until Wi‑Fi is fully restored before retrying the connection.
</Step>

<Step title="Verify your Wi‑Fi password">
Use your phone or laptop to join the same 2.4 GHz SSID and confirm the password is correct.
</Step>

<Step title="Test your internet connection">
Make sure other devices on the network can browse the internet. If they cannot, fix your internet service first.
</Step>

<Step title="Try an alternative network (mobile hotspot)">
Test with a mobile hotspot from your phone. If your device connects to the hotspot but not your home router, the router's settings likely need adjustment.

<Warning>
R+D devices require 2.4 GHz. Many modern phones default to 5 GHz for hotspots. Follow the steps below to enable 2.4 GHz on your phone.
</Warning>

<Tabs>
<Tab title="iPhone">
iPhones (12 and later) default to 5 GHz for Personal Hotspot, which R+D devices cannot use. To enable 2.4 GHz:

1. Open **Settings** → **Personal Hotspot**
2. Enable **Maximize Compatibility**
3. Turn on the hotspot

Without "Maximize Compatibility" enabled, your iPhone hotspot broadcasts at 5 GHz and R+D devices will not see it or connect.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Android">
Most Android phones default to 2.4 GHz, but some newer models may use 5 GHz. To check or change:

1. Open **Settings** → **Connections** (or **Network**) → **Mobile Hotspot**
2. Tap **Configure** or the settings icon
3. Look for **Band** or **AP Band** and select **2.4 GHz**

Menu names vary by manufacturer. Samsung uses "Mobile Hotspot," Google Pixel uses "Hotspot & tethering."
</Tab>
</Tabs>
</Step>

<Step title="Rule out device state issues">
- Ensure the Lockbox is not in Deep Sleep (Deep Sleep turns off Wi‑Fi). Learn more: <a href="/chastity-lockbox/technical-documentation/device-states/sleep-mode">Sleep Modes</a>.
- Restart the device and try again. Instructions: <a href="/chastity-lockbox/technical-documentation/device-states/restarting-device">Restarting Device</a>.
</Step>
</Steps>

## If you still can't connect

Use these advanced checks to resolve stubborn issues.

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Router and access point settings">
- Split 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz into separate SSIDs; disable band steering while testing.
- Security: use WPA2‑PSK (AES) or WPA2/WPA3 mixed. Avoid WPA3‑only, WEP, or WPA‑TKIP.
- Channel width: set 2.4 GHz to 20 MHz; use channels 1, 6, or 11 to reduce interference.
- Make sure DHCP is enabled and your network does not require static IP configuration for clients.
- Disable AP/client isolation and MAC address filtering for the device's SSID (or add the device to the allowlist).
- Ensure your firewall or parental‑control filters allow outbound HTTPS traffic to R+D services.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Lockbox shows a red circle">
Your Wi‑Fi is connected but the internet is unreachable. Power‑cycle your modem/router, verify your ISP connection, and confirm that your network does not require web‑page sign‑in (captive portal) for access.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Lockbox shows a persistent yellow circle">
Wait up to a minute after joining Wi‑Fi. If yellow persists, restart the device and the router, then reconnect on the 2.4 GHz SSID. If it still persists, try an alternate network (mobile hotspot) to rule out router configuration issues.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
