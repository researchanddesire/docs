<Info>
Terminology: You may see the terms Keyholder (the person with control) and Lockee (the person whose device is controlled) in some product areas, such as the Chastity Lockbox. Device sharing works the same regardless of the names you use for roles.
</Info>

## Prerequisites

- You have a dashboard account. If not, start with /dashboard/quick-start/registering-a-new-account
- You know your partner’s username or their email address
- Optional: Pair any devices you plan to share before inviting a partner

<Tip>
Sharing-related emails (invitations and connection updates) can be managed in your email preferences: /dashboard/settings/email-preferences
</Tip>

## Create a share request

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Partner settings">
Go to your <strong>Settings</strong> and open the <strong>Partners</strong> section.

<Check>
You should see an <strong>Invitations</strong> panel and your current <strong>Partners</strong> list.
</Check>
</Step>

<Step title="Create an invitation">
Click <strong>Create invitation</strong> in the top-right.
</Step>

<Step title="Choose how to invite">
<Tabs>
<Tab title="Invite by username">
Enter your partner’s exact username.

- Select <strong>Give access to my devices</strong> to let them control your devices.
- Select <strong>Request access to their devices</strong> to ask for control of theirs.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Invite by email">
Enter your partner’s full email address. If they don’t have an account yet, they’ll be prompted to register before accepting.

- Choose whether to <strong>Give access</strong> or <strong>Request access</strong>.
</Tab>
</Tabs>
</Step>

<Step title="Send and verify">
Send the invitation.

<Check>
- Your partner receives an email notification (if enabled)
- A matching entry appears under <strong>Pending invitations</strong>
</Check>
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
For privacy and security, we won’t confirm whether a username or email exists. Double-check spelling so your invitation reaches the right person.
</Note>

## Respond to invitations

When someone invites you to share device access, you receive:

1. An email notification (based on your email preferences)
2. A pending invitation in <strong>Settings → Pending invitations</strong>

Review each request carefully. Choose <strong>Accept</strong> to establish the connection or <strong>Decline</strong> to refuse access.

## Understand connection types

Each partner shows a clear relationship indicator in the Partners list:

- You’re sharing: you’ve given them control of your devices
- They’re sharing: they’ve given you control of their devices
- Mutual sharing: both partners can control each other’s devices

## Manage your connections

- Open the menu next to a partner’s name to manage the relationship
- Select <strong>Remove partner</strong> to end sharing in either direction
- Pending invites can be rescinded from the <strong>Pending invitations</strong> list

<Check>
After removal, the partner no longer appears in your Partners list and loses access to any shared devices.
</Check>

## Best practices

<Warning>
Only share device access with people you know and trust. You maintain full control of your sharing preferences and can remove access at any time.
</Warning>

- Align on expectations and boundaries before sharing
- Review your connected partners periodically
- Remove connections you no longer need

<Info>
If you’re using the Chastity Lockbox, assigning a Keyholder disables your own lock controls while sharing is active. For FAQs and safety features like Emergency Unlock, see:

- /chastity-lockbox/faqs
- /chastity-lockbox/technical-documentation/device-states/emergency-unlock
</Info>

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Troubleshooting">
- Invitation not received: ask your partner to check spam, confirm their address/username, and verify email notifications are enabled in /dashboard/settings/email-preferences
- Can’t find a username: invitations don’t confirm whether an account exists—send by email if you’re unsure
- Partner can’t accept: they must complete registration and email verification first
- Sent to the wrong person: rescind the pending invitation and re-send
</Accordion>
