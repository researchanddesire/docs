Devices such as the Deep Throat Trainer and Chastity Lockbox are sometimes shared between users—often in dynamics involving a top and a bottom. When multiple people have accessed the same device, ownership disputes can occur. This policy explains how we determine ownership and how to resolve disputes quickly and safely.

<Info>
  This policy governs device pairing and control within the Research and Desire
  Dashboard. It does not address returns, refunds, or warranty claims.
</Info>

## Definitions

- **Owner**: The account we recognize as having ultimate control over pairing and unpairing for a device.
- **Unpair**: The action of removing a device’s association with an account in the Dashboard. Once unpaired, any account can pair the device.

<Warning>
  Unpairing relinquishes ownership. Only unpair your device if you are prepared
  to give up control of it permanently.
</Warning>

## Primary ownership

The primary owner of a device is the individual who completed the purchase.

<Steps>
<Step title="Verify a purchase to claim ownership">
If we can verify a purchase through a Shopify order or internal store records and the purchaser’s email matches the one used to contact support, we designate that purchaser as the owner.

<Tip>
To speed up verification, contact support from the purchase email and include your order number if available.
</Tip>
</Step>

<Step title="Exercise owner rights">
The verified owner can request the device be unpaired from any account. No justification is required.

<Check>
After unpairing, the device is free to be paired by any account. Pairing it to a new account transfers ownership to that account.
</Check>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Ownership without a purchase record

If no purchase record can be verified, ownership is determined by device usage history.

<Steps>
<Step title="Identify initial owner by usage">
The first account to log an event from the device (for example, pairing, usage, or commands) is treated as the initial owner.
</Step>

<Step title="Request unpairing if needed">
That account holder may request the device be unpaired from any account at any time, for any reason. Support will process the unpairing immediately after verification of the requesting account.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Transferring ownership

Ownership can transfer in two ways.

### 1) Voluntary unpairing by the owner

- When the current owner unpairs the device from their account, this is an intentional release of ownership.
- The next account to pair the device becomes the new owner.

<Warning>
  Unpairing is irreversible from a policy standpoint. Once another user pairs
  the device, ownership transfers to that user.
</Warning>

<Info>
  When ownership history is unclear, Research and Desire assigns ownership to
  the original purchaser.
</Info>

### 2) Transfer of ownership by Research and Desire

If a device remains paired to the original purchaser’s account and they have not unpaired it, the requesting user can ask us to facilitate a transfer.

<Steps>
<Step title="We contact the purchaser">
We email the purchaser at the address on the original order.
</Step>

<Step title="30-day response window">
  The purchaser has 30 days to respond to the request.
</Step>

<Step title="Outcome">
- If the purchaser approves, we transfer ownership to the requesting user.
- If the purchaser declines, we do not transfer the device.
- If the purchaser does not respond within 30 days, we consider them non-responsive and transfer the device to the requesting user.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  This process respects the rights of original purchasers while providing a
  clear path forward for active users in unresolved disputes.
</Info>

<Info>
  Reinstalling or flashing firmware does not unpair a device. Pairing status
  persists through firmware reinstalls. If you need to change ownership, request

<Tip>
  If you no longer have access to the purchase email, contact support from the

## Summary of ownership flows

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Purchase record exists">
- The purchaser is the owner
- They can request unpairing at any time
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No purchase record">
  - The first account to log an event becomes the owner - They can request
  unpairing at any time
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Ownership transfer by unpairing">
  - Unpairing forfeits ownership - The next account to pair becomes the new
  owner - If history is unclear, R+D assigns the purchaser as owner
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Ownership transfer by R+D">
- R+D contacts the original purchaser
- If they approve or fail to respond within 30 days, the transfer proceeds
- If they decline, the transfer is denied
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Verification checklist

<Check>
Before you contact support, have the following ready:

- Email address associated with the purchase or your Dashboard account
- Order number (if available)
- Device model and serial or trainer ID (if visible)
- A brief description of the situation (e.g., “requesting unpair from all accounts”)
</Check>
